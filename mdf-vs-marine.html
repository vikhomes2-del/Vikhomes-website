Quick summary (for busy carpenters)

MDF — When to pick

Best for painted finishes, internal wardrobe backs, MDF doors and decorative panels. Smooth surface makes painting and veneering easy.

  • Cost-effective for non-structural parts
  • Excellent for paint and veneer
  • Easier to machine and cut for joinery

Marine Plywood — When to pick

Choose for structural strength, damp or outdoor-exposed areas, and where water resistance is needed (e.g., kitchens, boat furniture).

  • Superior strength and screw-holding
  • Water-resistant glue and better durability
  • Usually more expensive than MDF

How to decide — a simple workflow

  1. Is it structural? If yes → use Marine plywood.
  2. Will it be painted or veneered? If yes and not exposed to water → MDF gives the cleanest finish.
  3. Will it be exposed to moisture? If yes → always choose Marine plywood.
  4. Budget vs durability — for cabinets and wardrobe backs where strength isn't critical, MDF (15mm) offers big savings.
